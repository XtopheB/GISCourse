4a · Land productivity — yield (Prod_t / Area_ha)
Calculate yield (production ÷ area) as a new column. Use IIF(Area_ha > 0, Prod_t / Area_ha, 0) to avoid division by zero.
Use IIF to guard against zero area: IIF(Area_ha > 0, Prod_t/Area_ha, 0) AS yield
SELECT District, the_year, Crop, IIF(Area_ha > 0, Prod_t / Area_ha, 0) AS yield FROM Agriculture;
query.sql
4b · Economic productivity — value per hectare (Value_R / Area_ha)
Calculate economic productivity (value ÷ area) as a new column. Again guard against zero area.
Same pattern as 4a but divide Value_R by Area_ha: IIF(Area_ha > 0, Value_R/Area_ha, 0) AS ec_prod
SELECT District, the_year, Crop, IIF(Area_ha > 0, Value_R / Area_ha, 0) AS ec_prod FROM Agriculture;

Show districts with total production greater than 4000 t in year 2000.
Add HAVING SUM(Prod_t) > 4000 after GROUP BY. Remember: HAVING works on aggregated values; WHERE does not.
SELECT distr_id, District, the_year, SUM(Prod_t) AS total_prod FROM Agriculture WHERE the_year = 2000 GROUP BY distr_id, District, the_year HAVING SUM(Prod_t) > 4000;

8b · JOIN + WHERE + GROUP BY — low-poverty districts in 2000
Show total production in 2000 for districts with pov_rate < 0.3.
Join the two tables, filter with AND for the year and pov_rate, then GROUP BY distr_id, District, the_year.
SELECT distr_id, District, the_year, SUM(Prod_t) AS total_prod FROM Agriculture, pov_M2 WHERE distr_id = d_ID AND the_year = 2000 AND pov_rate < 0.3 GROUP BY distr_id, District, the_year;
